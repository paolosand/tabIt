# tabIt — Product Context

**register: product**

## Product purpose

tabIt turns a song (YouTube URL or audio file) into a play-along chord sheet: it detects the chords, key, and suggested scales, then follows along with playback so a guitarist can play in real time. The web app is the demo surface over the MIR engine; a Chrome extension follows later.

## Users

Guitarists and musicians, hobbyist to intermediate, who want to play along to songs they love without hunting for or paying for tabs. They read chord sheets, know what a key and a capo are, and value a chart they can glance at while their hands are on the instrument. Often sitting with the instrument, laptop nearby, in a relaxed practice setting.

## Brand / tone

Analog, tactile, honest. The interface should feel like a good songbook or a hand-kept music notebook, not a SaaS dashboard: warm paper, a real typeface, clear intent, nothing that screams "app." Calm and confident. Honest about uncertainty rather than pretending to be perfect (chord detection is imperfect; the UI says so gracefully).

## Anti-references (do NOT look like these)

- Generic SaaS chord tools and dashboards: cards-in-a-grid, gradient hero metrics, neon accents.
- "Music tech" clichés: neon-on-black, waveform-as-decoration, EQ-slider chrome, dark synthwave.
- Cluttered Ultimate-Guitar-style ad-heavy pages.
- Anything that reads as a developer tool. This is for musicians at rest, not engineers at work.

## Strategic principles

1. **The chord sheet is the hero.** The video is a small companion; the chart is what the eye lives on.
2. **Follow, don't just display.** The current chord is always obvious at a glance; the next chord is visible before it arrives (karaoke-style lookahead — free, since the whole chart is known up front).
3. **Honest confidence.** Low-confidence chords are visibly softer, not hidden or faked. Uncertainty is a feature, surfaced with grace. Users can correct a wrong chord.
4. **Analog over digital.** Every choice leans tactile and paper-like. Restraint over ornament.
5. **Lightweight and fast.** Paste, wait briefly, play. No accounts, no clutter, no friction.
